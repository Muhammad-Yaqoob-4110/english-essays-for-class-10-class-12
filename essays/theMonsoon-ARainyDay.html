The Monsoon/ A Rainy Day

"Nature, like man, sometimes weeps for gladness Vexed sailors curse the rain, for which poor shepherds pray in vain"

Rain is a great blessing for mankind. Monsoon is the wind that changes direction with the change of season. The monsoon prevails mainly in the Indian Ocean. The summer monsoon blows from southwest, generally from April to October It blows from Ocean to land. The summer monsoon usually causes heavy rainfall in the areas of the East Indies, India and Pakistan. It is the dominant climate event of the area. The pouring rain continues with the summer monsoon. It is called the rainy season in this area.

“In Monsoon, when there is rain, Rain and Rainbow together show beautiful of colours of nature."

Pakistan is an agricultural country and prosperity of our country so; farmers greatly depend on rain. If rains are sufficient then we have good agricultural crops and if they are scantily then famine occurs in many parts of country. On the other hand, if there are rains than needed, they bring ruination for the people. Thus, we can say that rains are both blessing and a curse for our countrymen. As it is said:

"Without rain there is no life.”
But,

“Excess of everything is harmful.”

In the summer season, when it is very hot the people pray for rains so that they can get relief from scorching heat of the sun. At such times when the clouds appear on the sky everybody becomes happy.

Last Sunday was probably the hottest day of this summer. The summer moved like a flame upon plains and meadows. Suddenly, the gusts of monsoon came like a sudden surge of grace. The sky was overcast with dark and silvery clouds. A cool refreshing wind began to fan everywhere. The thunder and lightning were accompanied with heavy rain.

“The sound of the rain needs no translation."

The joy of people knew no bounds. It must have rained for three hours. All the streets were flooded. It looked as if there was water and water everywhere. There was knee deep water on certain roads and low-lying areas in the city. The traffic on these roads had been closed for some time. As it is said:

"Without the rain We would never feel thankful for warmth of the Sun.”

When the rain stopped, ultimately the people started moving. The traffic which was standing still now started moving on the roads. The people who had taken shelter under the roofs of shops and buildings now started moving towards their destination. Rain made all the people happy as they had got rid of scorching heat of summer. Thus, rain is a true blessing.

“Rain , rain , rain, don’t go away,

Rain , rain , rain, I hope you will stay,

Rain , rain , rain, come again and again.”

Of course, the rain also brought misery to certain people. Many huts in the low-lying slums were washed away. Many houses collapsed due continuous rains. The roofs of many old houses started leaking. The streets and the roads had become slippery. Thus, people had to face much trouble. No doubt the trees looked as if they were washed by nature. The whole surrounding looked green and pleasant. The farmers were also very happy as they hoped to have good crops now. Even the animals and the birds were full of joy and mirth. As it is said:

"Green is the prime color of the world, and that from which its loveliness arises.”
