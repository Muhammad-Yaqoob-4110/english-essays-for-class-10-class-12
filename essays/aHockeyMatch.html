A Hockey Match

There is a sensation and delight not only in playing games but also in witnessing them. We can see the spectators absorbed in the matches. The people can enjoy the games and forget the worries of their life. The beginners can learn the art of games when they examine the performance of the professionals. There goes a good proverb,

"All work and no play make jack a dull boy.”

Sports refer activities to the physical generally Involving skills competitive nature. An individual or group often competes against another for entertainment. The importance of sports in our life is very much. Games and sports are not only important for success in studio but it is important for success in every walk of our life.

“Sports can create hope where once there was only despair. It is more powerful than government in breaking down racial barriers.”

(Nelson Mandala)

Games and sports include all indoor and outdoor games and also athletics. In ancient Greek, they formed the principal part of education. In the advanced countries of the present day, also they are a regular feature of school and college curriculum.

The great virtue that sports teach is to acquire the will to win. If anyone knows how to acquire the will to win, he will succeed in every aspect of life. The willing power of the sportsman is increased by winning the game. Comparatively, a weak-willed man resigns himself after defeat but a true sportsman who is defeated must strengthen his nerve. Because he knows:

“Failure is the pillar to success."

So, great efforts with strong determination helps him to win any game. I and my friends made a program to witness a hockey match. As it is said:

“Hockey is the original extreme sport."

I was delighted to witness a hockey match between Pakistan and Germany at the Pakistan Stadium recently. The players of both teams showed their qualities of strength, stamina, agility and mobility. As it is Said:

"Hockey players have fire in their hearts and ice in their veins."

The Germans dominated in the beginning but our players fought back and coordinated remarkably. The Germans made strong attempts but goalkeeper made spectacular saves. The match was suspended briefly when home crowd fired crackers which cause dense clouds of smoke all around.

The Pakistani forwards kept the Possession and played like a team. The only weakness however, was in the penalty corners. The team played with full devotion. Pakistan opened its attacks mostly from the flanks but the speed and timely interception of the Germans attackers saved them. As it is said:

“It takes effort to win a game but it takes courage to change the game.”

Our left half took the ball from the German attackers and after running up a few paces sent in through pass to inside left. He dispatched the ball towards the goal with a flick. Another Pakistani player who was closely following him, tapped the ball to score the goal. At last, the time was up and our team came out happy and victorious. They had won the match by one goal. We clapped and shouted with joy. Our players had not let us down. The crowd went wild with joy at the victory of Pakistan. As it is said:

"Hockey is a man's game. The team with most real men wins.”
