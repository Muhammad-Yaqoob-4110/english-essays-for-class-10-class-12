Village Life

Village life is the scenery of the changing seasons and divine beauty. It also depicts the moral and cultural values of a state or a province in their true colours and aspects.

"God made the country and man-made the town"
(Cowper)

“A simple life is its own reward"

Man is a social animal. He cannot live alone. Therefore, he wants to live with his fellowmen. So, he forms communities, villages, towns, cities, and countries. A village is a small unit. In village, we can observe nature more closely. A poet presents his thoughts about village in the following words. Sweet Album

“Loveliest village of plain.

Where health and plenty

Cheered swain labouring

Where smiling spring

Its earliest visit paid

And parting Summer

Lingering bloom delayed”

village is a small town situated in a country area. It consists of a few small huts and mud houses. These houses are built all over the area without any order. It is the surrounding greenery of the village that makes its wonderful. The tall trees, green fields, murming streams, flowery bushes and calm nature add to the beauty of village. As it is said:

“In village Nature is quite open, abundant and generous.”

There is no special fun for the villagers. There only hobby is to make gossips and watch TV in the evening. Their marriage ceremony is the most amusing event of the village. This is the occasion when the villagers show their feelings of joy and pleasure “Village fair” is still another event of amusement for the them.

There are many benefits of village life. First, life in a village is an easy and pure. The needs of the villagers are a few. Eggs, milk, vegetables and butter are their common diet. Their diet is pure though it is simple. Their dresses, food, customs and manners are simple but decent.

“Nothing is simpler than greatness indeed to be simple is to be great.”

I cannot even envisage of walking in the roads in the city because there is so much noise of people and the cars honk incessantly. The living experience in the village is worthy of claim. There is a lot peace village life is about purity.

"The sun does not forget a village just because it is small.”

(African Proverb)

Second, the villagers are sincere and honest. They know no selfishness. They love their customs and traditions. They are simple, honest, innocent, kind and hospitable. There are also some disadvantages of village life. It lacks modern facilities. The hospitals and education are limited there. Most of the people in villages are ignorant. Due to their ignorance, they face many problems.

“Everything which makes for sincerity is a further step towards true culture.”

The villagers are content with their lot. They do not have high ambitions. Their only interest is how to increase the yield of crops. Villagers are very simple. They do not know any tactics and selfishness. They live for others.

In Pakistan more than 60% of the population lives in rural areas. Moreover, in our economy village plays a very dominant role in contribution to GDP , food supply of raw materials to industries. Hence, the government should spread Mass education by establishing schools and colleges. Roads and other means of communication should be improved. Villages should be electrified. Rural banking should be setup for financial assistance to the village people. If these steps are taken, the villagers would play their role in the development of the country.

“No country can make through progress without the progress of its villages.”

The poet has summed up all this in these lines:

“Without proper transportation Without supply of electricity. Life here is so different Deprived of city like facility”

In short, it is true that country life is sweet one. Many poets have sung the praise of it. Great men and thinkers have spent their invaluable lives in the country side. Hence, village life has been cherished highly, in all ages by every soulful man.

"Beauty is truth, truth beauty that’s all.

Ye know on earth, and all ye need to know."
(John Keats)
