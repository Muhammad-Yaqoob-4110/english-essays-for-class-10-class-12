Quaid-e-Azam

"Lives of great men all remind us we can make our lives sublime and departing leave behind us foot-prints on the sands of time.”

(Long Fellow)

A great man is one who serves humanity heart and soul. But no man is great who is great only his lifetime. The of greatness is the page of history. History is replete with noble and sacred aim of life was to help and guide their fellow men. We read and recall commemorate and command the names of great scientists, artists, politicians, poet’s sages whose tireless and selfless efforts brought comfort and peace to the suffering humanity. They died but their death makes them immortal because they live in our hearts. Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah is one of them.

“Personality is an indefinable thing, a strange force that has power over the souls of men."

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was a unique leader of Indian Muslims. He offered Muslims an unprecedented leadership. No one before or after him practiced politics as he did. Allah has treasured up all the supreme qualities of a great leader in him. When the Muslims of sub-continent made up their minds for emancipation, they turned their eyes on Quaid-e-Azam who cut off the fetters of slavery and brought his people the gift of freedom. He had qualities of a great hero by all standards. In other words:

"His personality is a beautiful blend of intelligence, determination, faith, morality and velour.”

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born in Karachi on December 25th, 1876. He got his early education in a local Maktab. He was a very intelligent student. Then he got admission in Missionary High School. After that he made towards England for Bar-at-Law. When he was in England, Muslims besought him to come to India and let Indian Muslims through the fire and water to their destination. The Muslims were placed between fires; The English and the Hindus. It was the Quaid who freed them from dual slavery.

"Fame is the perfume of heroic deeds."
(Socrates)

He worked as a member of Indian National Congress. He wanted to unite Hindus and Muslims against English so that India could win freedom. But soon he realized that Hindus were trying to safeguard their own interest, negating the interest of the Muslims. So, he joined Muslim League to fight for the rights of the Muslims. Mr. Jinnah also fought for the rights of the Muslims.

"Quality, not quantity, should be the measure of one's character."

(Douglas Jerrold)

Quaid-e-Azam devoted his life to Muslims. His leadership kindled a new imagination among the frustrated Muslims. He created political consciousness among the Muslims of India. It was due to his leadership that Muslims regained their confidence. Each and every Muslim responded to clarion call and they, with one Voice demanded for them. Quaid e Azam said:

“India is neither a country nor its inhabitants a nation. This is a Subcontinent where many nations live.”

He further said:

“We believe in one God; they believe in many Gods. We don't have caste system; they have caste system. How can we live together?”

This sharp minded, hawk eyed, unflinching and unyielding man worked day and night for Pakistan. It was due to his leadership that the British could not weld the Hindus and the Muslims into one unity. Ultimately, he was able to carve out Pakistan on the map of the world. The dream Pakistan became reality on 14 August, 1947 1947. Quaid-e-Azam became the first Governor General of Pakistan.

“Personality is to man what perfume is to a flower.”

(Charles Lamb)

Quaid-e-Azam was against all social evils. He wanted to see Pakistan a peaceful, powerful and prosperous Country. He died on 11th September, 1948. But his courage, hard work fearlessness, oratory and his services for Muslims will keep him alive in our hearts.

Quaid-e-Azam was a dynamic leader. Quaid-e-Azam will always live in the hearts of the Pakistanis as the father of the nation. His words, "Faith, Unity and Discipline" will always inspire the people in their struggle for nation- building.

“A man of courage is also full of faith."
(Cicero)
