My Last Day at School

"The quality of a school is measured more by the kind of student it turns out than the kind it takes in."

(Robert J. Kibbee)

There is hardly any living being who has not gone through ups and downs of life. Actually, life is full of bad as well as good incidents. Some of them may be forgotten, whereas others leave an ever-lasting imprint on the mind.

“Write the bad things that are done to you in sand, but good things that happen to you on a piece of marble.”

Helen Keller, the dumb and deaf American authoress, holds that we do not value the things until we lose them. Her words echoed in my mind on my last day at college. It was only then I realized how much I cherished the school I was going to leave. It seemed to me as if somebody were drawing my soul out from my body.

“School is a place where we spend the most precious time of our Life, childhood."

School is a sacred place. It educates the young people. It is the place where concrete foundations of a nation are laid down apparently. It imparts education, but secretly it grooms the young generation. For life and its weighty responsibilities. Students spend a substantial part of their lives in their schools. As it is said,

“School is a is a place where stones are carved into diamonds.”

Therefore, the students love their schools like their homes. As it is said,

“It is natural for human to love the place, where they spend some time.”

Students develop an emotional association with their schools. Moreover, in schools’ students live like a family So, it is difficult for them, to separate from each other. But a day comes, when they have to desert the school and all its charms. It is the time of real sadness for them. But they pass this ordeal with great patience. As it is said,

“Patience is the half part of faith."

In the store house of my memories, my last day at school occupies a prominent position. I cannot forget the day when left the school. School is a place where we spend ten golden years of our life. Those happy days, the lectures and the punishment on loosing test still haunt my memories. School is a place where we form many associations.

I also remember my last day school. It was a pleasant day of "3rd March" But it was a gloomy day for me and my class mates.

My friends and I were aware of the grim reality that we were about to leave the school forever. Throughout, our stay we had been so excited and cheerful. We never entertained that a day would come to separate us were so emotional that they could not 2 control themselves and started weeping. Some had tears in their eyes, but they controlled themselves. As it is said,

“Tears are the Silent language of grief.”

We decided to depart in the same joyful mood, we had been there for last ten years. First of all, we planned to meet our teachers. We obtained their autographs in our diaries. Then, we had a round of school to see every nook and corner of it. I went to my own class. My mind flew back about the noisy classes. School canteen was full of life as usual. Then, I walked to library. I had a look on the countless books which I could not read. I had tears in my eyes and I remembered these lines:

“Tears from depth of some divine despair,

Rise in the heart and gather to eyes.

In looking on happy autumn fields

And thinking of the days that are no more.”

Then we met our headmaster/ headmistress who gave character certificates. He advised us to be honest and hardworking in life and wished a continued success for our future. He/ She also advised us that,

“Being honest never harms anyone but being a liar only hurts you”

In the evening, we got ready to go our homes. It was the time to say goodbye to each other. We embraced each other for the last time. Some of us could not control themselves and began to weep bitterly. Finally, we departed with the hope that we would meet again someday, somewhere. This is how I spent my last day at school.

“Farewell my friends! Farewell my Foes!

My love with these, My care with those.”
