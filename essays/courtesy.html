Courtesy

“Verily, the Muslim with perfect faith is the one whose manners are the best.”

Courtesy means showing good behaviour and polite manners. Its source is “kindness” and “affection”. The person who shows courtesy is called “Courteous person”. As it is said:

“A tree is known by its fruit, A man by his deeds, A good deed is never lost. A man who shows courtesy reaps friendship and a man who plants kindness gathers love.”

He always shows polite behaviour and kind manners. He takes much care of the feelings of the others. He respects others and is respected by others. As it is said:

“Small courtesies sweeten the life, greater ennoble it.”

Courtesy may be set in contrast with “tyranny” If tyranny is the greatest evil, courtesy is the cardinal virtue of society. As tyranny is the root of all “vices”, courtesy is the root of all virtues. As it is said:

“Courtesy costs nothing, but buys everything.”

Courtesy is common to all the religions of the world. All religions teach and propagate morality and courtesy. It is the foundation stone of all social, cultural and political movements. It is said:

“All doors open to courtesy.”

Therefore, it is the most sacred duty of every human to teach and practice courtesy. As it is said:

“Life is so short but that there is always enough time for courtesy. Love begets love and politeness begets politeness.”

People should be urged to better their manners. They should be motivated to improve their conduct and behaviour with other people. The prime purpose of education should be to make humans courteous, well-mannered and polite. We can spread courtesy by writing good books and spreading them throughout the world. We can develop courtesy by delivering sermons. As it is said:

“Courtesy is a silver lining around a dark cloud of civilization. It is one of the best parts of refinement and in many ways, an art of heroic beauty in the vast gallery of man’s cruelty and baseness.”

It makes the life of a man maintained. The life of a man should be composed of strong wills and truth. A man with such qualities does not become slave of anyone. Because,

“The strength of character can turn enemies into friends.”

A courteous person is not so passionate. He is not prejudice to anyone. He does not make the use of oily tongue. He follows the rules of politeness and kindness. As it is said:

Life without courtesy is like:

“A man without heart.

An eye without sight.

Flower without smell

and A skull without brain.”

A courteous person always prays good for others, even for his enemies. He keeps his ears away from rumours. He dislikes flattery. He respects others and is respected by others. He is kind and polite to everyone. Our Holy Prophet (PBUH) was Himself a courteous person. Hazrat Ayesha (RA) said:

“His (PBUH) morals and characters are embodiment of the Holy Quran.”

Moreover, she (RA) said:

“The behaviour of the Holy Prophet (PBUH) is the Holy Quran.”

That was the reason that He (PBUH) succeeded in preaching “Islam” throughout the world. He (PBUH) was the most influential person in the world. The people attracted towards Him (PBUH) due to His (PBUH) polite behaviour and kind manners. We should strictly follow the rules of courtesy to make our earth paradise. As it is said:

“Little deeds of kindness

Little words of love

Make our earth an Eden

Like the heaven above.”

All the Prophets (AS) of Allah propagated courtesy by their sermons and kind deeds. They were kind, polite and courteous due to which people entered the circle of “Islam”. Actually,

“Politeness, courtesy and kindness were the qualities of Prophets (AS)

Courtesy, motivates the people to mend their thoughts. There are many benefits of courtesy. As it is said:

“The grace of Allah is courtesy.”

First, it provides us tremendous joy and satisfaction. Second, it makes man popular not only with humans but also with animals. Third, it makes the people best preachers of the world. Our Holy Prophet (SAWW) was Himself so courteous and He (PBUH) gave much importance to courtesy. Our Holy prophet (PBUH) said:

"The best amongst ye is the one those manners are the best."

Good manners are infectious. They pass from one person to others and develop the society. If we are polite to others, they will be polite to us and influence the whole society lack of courtesy makes life sad and dull. As it is said,

“The most insufferable man is the one who lacks the basic courtesy.”

Courtesy is such a great virtue that it can turn the enemies into friends. Therefore, we should endeavour, to be courteous and kind to other people. We should also preach courtesy in society.

“So, ‘please’ and ‘thank you’ are the words with which we can keep the machine of life running sweetly.”
