The Scene at a Railway Station

The train drawn by the heavy engine would stop briefly for the passengers at places called railway stations. The British invented the railway and locomotive. The train-track was laid for the trains to connect different towns and villages on the route. People come to the railway station to leave their dears but some people come to receive their dears. The people who leave their dears become sad and who receive their dears they become happy. Therefore,

"Railway station is a place which presents a blend of feelings of sorrows and joys.”

Last Sunday, I went to the railway station with my father. He has a job there. I saw typical scenes there. The smart uniformed station master and his staff hurriedly move about when some train is due in or out. The platform is thronged with passengers, men, women and children. Some stay close to their piles of luggage other just walk about looking at colorful ads. The vendors do roaring business. Their brightly lit stalls or barrows at different points attract the passenger’s. There is a noise and clamor all around.

“A railway station obviously has typical scenes.”

Meals, fruits. Gol gappa’s, pakoras, books, magazines, souvenirs and Knick- knacks sell like hot cakes. In summer, pitchers and fans are in great demand. Watercoolers and taps are the busiest spots. There was great crowd.

“The prospect of a long journey ahead worries the passengers.”

As soon as the announcement about the approaching train is made, a frantic activity emerges at the station. People rush towards the ticket room. The passengers sitting in the waiting room take their luggage’s and come out. Some begin to stare in the direction of the arriving train. At last, the whistle of the train is heard. The train looks graceful when it enters the station. As soon as it stops, people emerge from the interior of the train. Those who are out, try to get on the train to оссuру the seats.

Some are seen to be dragging their suitcases and bags. There is a great hustle and bustle on the platform. Some passengers rush to the tea stalls. Some are seen carry their luggage and seen buying eatables. Some rush to the water coolers to fill their water bottles and containers with cool water. The vendors shout slogans to attract the attention of the passengers to their things.

There are a few policemen roaming about on the platform. A few ticket checkers dressed in white uniforms also come into view. Some passengers are seen requesting ticket checkers for the booking of the seats. There are other people who have come either to receive their friends or see them off. They are seen to be embracing each other. Then suddenly, the train whistles. The guard begins to wave green banner. The passengers rush towards their compartments. The train begins to creep slowly. People begin to run with the creeping train. They try to get on the running train. Those who have come to see off their dear ones stand on the platform and wave their hands. Their faces are sad and depressed. As soon as train disappears, they begin to leave the station. Soon, all the activity which started so rapidly a little while ago end so suddenly. The station is deserted a last. Thus,

"A railway station is a place of great interest."
